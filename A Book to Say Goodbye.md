# A Book to Say Goodbye

*For the version of me who stayed too long.*  
*For the version of me who is finally leaving with grace.*

---

## A Note Before You Begin

This is not a book about anger.

It is about loving someone who slowly stopped choosing me, and finally choosing myself.

I am writing this book after noticing everything. After the silence. After the waiting. After seeing your words but not your actions. After understanding that I was holding on to something that was already gone.

I wrote this late at night, alone, when my heart was tired but my eyes were finally open.

If you are reading this, I hope you understand.

**Please, after reading this book, do not ever try to contact me.**

I am done with waiting. I am done with expecting. I am leaving you.

I hope you find someone better in life. I mean that.

I hope I become successful in my life. That is what I am working toward now.

Maybe one day, when I am successful, you will try to reach out to me.

But at that time, I will have no time for you.

I wish that happens.

This is my goodbye. Not to love. To staying where I was not chosen.

---

# Chapter One
## The Beginning

I remember everything.

Not just the big moments. The small ones too.

We met once. You picked me up from my office. I remember that day clearly.

We spent three to four hours at Port Grand. The time did not feel long. It felt like it belonged to us.

I remember when I was going home. I told you, *"Main wapas ja rahi thi"*. I was leaving. And you said, *"Haan, tu wapas ja rahi thi"*. Yes, you were leaving. Such a small conversation. But I still carry it.

Before New Zealand, we met in the morning. I had a standup meeting at work. I knew I had to attend. I did not. I chose you instead. That is who I was with you. You first. Always.

You were not just someone I liked. You were someone I made room for, in my day, my time, my choices.

I do not give my attention easily. I do not let people in unless they meet my standards. You met them. Or I believed you did.

In the beginning, there was warmth. There was interest. There was the feeling that someone far away still made space for me.

We lived in different countries and different time zones. But at first, distance felt small. When you messaged, my evening changed. When you called, I moved my plans. When you needed me, I showed up.

I made time for you. Always.

For a while, it felt mutual. That is what beginnings do. They make you believe.

Every day I prayed for you. I said your name to Govind. I said: *Please be with him. Every time.*

I asked people about accounts officer job openings for you. It did not work out the way I hoped. But I tried. I did my very best.

I did not keep score. I just gave, because that is how I love.

---

# Chapter Two
## The Person I Made Time For

For you, I changed my priorities. Work could wait. Sleep could wait. Meetings could wait.

I postponed calls and plans because you were free and I did not want to miss you. I sometimes cancelled things I had already promised, not because I was careless, but because I meant it when I cared.

I do not regret that I loved you. I regret that I loved you alone.

I gave you fast replies, random updates, attention, listening. Not grand gestures. The ordinary daily texture of love. The dumb questions, the random thoughts, the laughter that showed up without being asked.

I showed up. I was there. I was real.

You may not have seen the weight of that while you had it. But those small things were not small. They were how I loved.

For you, I broke many of my own boundaries. I gave you a level of priority I had never given anyone before.

When you called, I came. When you needed me, I was there. When you went quiet, I waited with a patience that was less virtue and more fear.

Looking back, I regret abandoning my own boundaries and self-respect. Not because loving you was wrong. Because forgetting myself in the process was.

Love should never feel like constantly asking to be noticed. I had been begging without using the word. Not with my voice. With my patience. With every message that went unanswered for hours, then days.

I thought I was being understanding. I thought love required waiting gracefully for someone who kept asking me to wait.

It does not.

---

# Chapter Three
## Distance and Silence

People love to blame distance. It turns complicated human choices into geography, as though love were a matter of maps rather than intention.

We woke up when the other was falling asleep. I will not pretend that was easy. But distance was never the real problem.

The real problem was simpler: I was always willing to bridge the gap, and you were gradually less willing to meet me in the middle.

Time zones do not cause silence. People do.

When someone wants to talk to you, they find a way. A message before work. A voice note on a commute. Ten minutes of genuine connection is worth more than an empty day of pretending you were too occupied to reach out.

You were busy. You were tired. You would reply later. Later became tomorrow. Tomorrow became next week. Next week became a kind of limbo where I existed in your life only when it was convenient for you to remember I was there.

And still, I made time for you. I woke up early. I stayed up late. I treated your schedule like scripture and mine like something negotiable.

That was not romance. That was self-erasure dressed in devotion.

Some goodbyes happen long before people actually leave. You did not leave all at once. You left in increments. A shorter reply. A cancelled call. A silence that grew until silence became the pattern.

I kept waiting for the old you to return. The person who made me feel chosen in the beginning was real. The person who gradually stopped choosing me was real too. I spent too long honoring the first while excusing the second.

I became an expert in your excuses. You were busy with work. You were stressed. You needed space. Some of these may have been true. But truth is not the same as effort.

A person can be overwhelmed and still send a message that takes five seconds. Silence is also a message.

> *"It says a lot about how they feel about you. Listen to their actions, not their words."*

I spent months listening to your words. *I miss you. I'm just busy. Things will go back to normal soon.* Words are easy. I wanted so badly to believe them that I stopped paying attention to what your behavior was telling me every day.

Your actions said you had time for everything except me. Your actions said I was the one who always reached out, always waited, always adjusted.

I only saw your words. I never saw your actions. I am done confusing the two.

> *"Sub ka sub ke bagair guzara ho hi jata, yeh sirf hamara waham hota hai ke hum khaas hain."*
>
> *Everyone gets by without everyone else. It is only our illusion that we are special.*

You will be fine without me. You probably already are. And I am learning to be fine without you too. Being special to someone is not the same as being chosen by them.

---

# Chapter Four
## Learning My Worth Again

There is a moment after heartbreak when you stop asking, *Why did they leave?* and start asking, *Why did I stay?*

That question changed everything for me. I could not control your interest. I could not force your consistency. I could not love you into loving me back. But I could choose what I accepted. I could choose myself.

Learning my worth again has not been sudden. Some days I almost text you. Some days I rehearse explanations for your behavior as though understanding it might bring you back. Then I remember.

I tied my worth to your replies. To your calls. To the temporary warmth of your attention when you decided to offer it. And every time you withdrew, I felt my value withdraw with you.

It is not measured that way. It never was.

Someone else's inability to choose me consistently is not a reflection of my insufficiency. It is a reflection of their capacity, their timing, their priorities. I can hold compassion for that without making it my home.

You were not a villain. You were someone who gradually stopped wanting what I was still giving. I was someone who kept giving anyway.

I deserve someone who is as excited to talk to me as I am to talk to them. I deserve consistent intention. Mutual effort. The kind of love that feels like rest, not like work.

I was never too much. I was never asking for too much. I was giving the right kind of love to someone who no longer had the will to meet me there.

> *"Standards are not arrogance. Boundaries are not attitude. Clarity is attractive."*

I saved those words at twelve-forty in the morning on a night when my heart felt too loud. They were not a rebuke. They were a reminder that the way I love is not the problem. The way I stopped requiring that love to be returned was.

I am rebuilding my boundaries now. Not to keep people out. To keep myself in.

I know my worth. Not because a book told me to. Because I finally lived the opposite and understood the cost.

---

# Chapter Five
## What I Wish You Knew

If I could tell you one thing: I remember everything, and it was never casual for me.

I remember you picking me up from my office. I remember Port Grand, three or four hours that still live in me. I remember the morning before New Zealand, when I skipped my standup just to meet you. I remember praying for you. Saying your name to Govind. Asking about job openings for you.

The time was real. The care was real. The love was real, even if yours was already fading while mine was still growing.

The fast replies. The random messages. The attention. The way I listened. The way I put you first. Those were not performances. That was love.

I wish you knew that I saw the shift. I was not blind. I was hopeful. There is a difference.

I wish you had been honest. If you had said you did not feel the same anymore, I would have cried. But I would not have spent months reading silence like a puzzle. Honesty would have hurt less.

I wish you knew distance was never our real problem. Indifference was.

I do not hate you. I hope you are well. I hope you find what you were looking for.

I choose myself now. Not because you were nothing. Because I was forgetting who I was.

> *"I don't stay in places where I have to keep asking for clarity."*

That is not a threat. It is a standard. And I am finally living up to my own.

---

# Chapter Six
## The Goodbye Letter

*Written at 12:40 AM, on a night when the world was asleep and I was finally awake.*

---

Dear you,

I loved you. I want that to be the first line, not hidden, not softened.

I remember you picking me up from my office. Port Grand. Skipping my standup before New Zealand. Praying for you every day, your name, Govind, and the same request: *Please be with him.* Trying to find job openings for you, asking people again and again.

You mattered to me. You were never casual. You were never small in my life.

I do not regret loving you. I regret that I stopped loving myself in the process.

Somewhere between your first message and your last silence, I forgot that love is not supposed to feel like waiting in a room where only one person keeps opening the door. I forgot that making time for someone who cannot make time for you is not devotion, it is disappearance.

I saw it happening. The slow fade. The shorter replies. The cancelled calls. The excuses that sounded reasonable until I realized I was the only one who needed them to be reasonable.

I do not blame you. Feelings change. People drift. You did not owe me forever. What is done is done.

What remains is me. And I am finally ready to stop living in the space between your last message and your next one.

We lived in different countries, but distance was never the real problem. The problem was that I was always willing to bridge the gap, and you were gradually less willing to meet me there.

When someone genuinely wants to talk to you, sending a message only takes a few seconds. I learned that the hard way.

I always made time for you. Looking back, I regret abandoning my own boundaries. Not because you asked me to, but because I forgot that no love is worth that price.

Some goodbyes happen long before people actually leave. This is mine.

This is not a goodbye born from anger. It is a thank-you for what was real between us. I gave you the best version of me I knew how to give.

Letting you go is the hardest decision I have made. But it is also my last act of love, the kind that does not beg, does not chase, does not wait in a door that only I am still standing in.

I showed up. I was there. I was real.

If you can go twenty-four hours without texting me, if you can go another twenty-four hours without showing up, then you can go a whole lifetime without me. And I can live a whole lifetime without you.

I am sorry. Not for loving you. Not for trying. But for staying in a place where I kept believing your words while your actions told me everything I needed to know.

We both deserve someone better. Someone who does not make love feel like waiting. Someone whose presence matches their promises.

So yeah. Goodbye.

---

I am writing this after noticing everything.

Please do not contact me after reading this book. Not once. Not to explain. Not to apologize. Not to ask why.

I am done with waiting. I am done with expecting. I am leaving you.

I hope you find someone better in life. I mean that.

I hope I become successful in my life. That is my focus now.

Maybe one day, when I am successful, you will try to reach out to me.

But at that time, I will have no time for you.

I wish that happens.

---

Choosing myself does not look like the movies. It looks like not sending the message. It looks like deleting the draft. It looks like believing that consistent intention is not too much to ask for.

I still hope I find someone who makes room for me in the middle of everything else. Someone who checks in. Someone who keeps their word. Someone who chooses me the way I was always willing to choose them.

That hope is not about you. It is about me.

---

### Epilogue
#### The Final Goodbye

Thank you for the beginning. Thank you for Port Grand. For the morning before New Zealand. For every memory I still carry.

I release you now. Not from memory. From the part of me that kept waiting.

We were special to each other for a time. That time mattered.

But I am done waiting.

Please do not contact me after reading this book.

I hope you find someone better in life. I hope I become successful in my life.

Maybe one day you will try to reach out to me when I am at my best. But at that time, I will have no time for you. I wish that happens.

Goodbye.

---

*End.*
